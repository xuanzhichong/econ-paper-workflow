# Verified Citation Workflow for Economics Papers

## Principle

Never cite a paper, working paper, or institutional report that you cannot verify.

## Preferred Verification Order

1. DOI or journal page
2. Crossref record
3. publisher page
4. RePEc / IDEAS / NBER / AEA listing
5. Google Scholar as a discovery aid, not as the final authority

## Working Paper Discipline

When citing working papers:

- record the series and number when available
- note if there is a later journal version
- do not cite both versions as if they were different papers unless there is a reason

## What To Check

- author list
- year
- title
- venue or series
- DOI if published
- working paper number if unpublished

## Common Failures

- citing an early working-paper title after the paper is published under a new title
- mixing journal and working-paper years
- using SSRN, NBER, and journal versions inconsistently
- citing a secondary summary instead of the source paper

## Minimum Rule

Use at least two independent sources when the citation is central to the paper's contribution or factual claim.
