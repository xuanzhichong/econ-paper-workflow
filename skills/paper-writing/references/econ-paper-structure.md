# Empirical Economics and Agricultural Economics Paper Structure

Use this as the default structure for empirical economics and agricultural economics papers.

This is a default structure, not a rigid formula.  
The organizing principle should always be: **the paper structure should make the identification logic, empirical evidence, and contribution easy to follow**.

---

## Default Structure

1. Introduction
2. Institutional background / context
3. Data
4. Empirical strategy / identification
5. Main results
6. Mechanisms / heterogeneity
7. Robustness
8. Conclusion
9. Appendix

When needed, sections may be combined or renamed, especially in shorter papers.  
However, avoid collapsing identification into vague `methods` language or burying core design logic in the appendix.

---

## Section Intent

### 1. Introduction

Purpose:

- motivate the question
- state why it matters economically or for policy
- explain what the literature already knows
- state what remains unresolved
- preview the identification strategy
- summarize the main finding at an appropriate level of caution
- state the contribution relative to the literature
- preview the structure of the paper

The introduction should promise only what the design can support.

---

### 2. Institutional Background / Context

Purpose:

- explain the setting, policy, market, or rule change
- describe timing, eligibility, rollout, or treatment assignment
- show why this setting generates useful empirical variation
- provide only the context needed to understand the design

This section should support identification, not become a general background essay.

---

### 3. Data

Purpose:

- define the sample and core variables
- explain sources, merges, and restrictions
- describe the unit of observation and time window
- clarify treatment, outcomes, and controls
- explain key sample exclusions, missingness rules, and transformations
- provide enough information for the reader to understand how the estimation sample is formed

The data section should make the empirical sample and variable construction legible, even if full detail is deferred to the appendix.

---

### 4. Empirical Strategy / Identification

Purpose:

- present the estimating framework
- define the source of identifying variation
- state assumptions and threats
- explain fixed effects, controls, clustering, and weights where relevant
- clarify why the design supports the intended interpretation
- preview the diagnostics or falsification exercises that address main threats

This section should make the logic of the design explicit rather than leaving it implicit in the results section.

---

### 5. Main Results

Purpose:

- present the baseline estimates first
- explain sign, magnitude, and economic meaning
- link each headline claim to a table or figure
- show how the estimate changes across meaningful specifications
- keep the ordering identification-driven rather than decorative

A typical internal ordering is:

1. baseline estimates  
2. identification support or dynamic evidence  
3. interpretation of economic magnitude  

Main results should carry the central empirical claim.  
Do not let mechanism, heterogeneity, or robustness displace the core design.

---

### 6. Mechanisms / Heterogeneity

Purpose:

- provide theory-motivated extensions to help interpret the main effect
- distinguish supporting evidence from the baseline result
- explain where effects are stronger, weaker, or likely to operate through particular channels
- keep claims narrower than or equal to what the design supports

This section should support interpretation, not create a second main paper inside the paper.

---

### 7. Robustness

Purpose:

- test the main design’s weak spots
- organize checks by identification threat
- show whether the substantive conclusion survives alternative samples, definitions, inference choices, or design-sensitive specifications
- separate useful checks from decorative appendix accumulation

Robustness should answer: **what concern does this check address?**

---

### 8. Conclusion

Purpose:

- restate the main finding at the right level of strength
- summarize the contribution
- discuss limitations honestly
- distinguish evidence from policy extrapolation
- state broader implications without overselling

The conclusion should not become a second introduction or a place for stronger claims than the paper earns.

---

### 9. Appendix

Purpose:

- house robustness variants, extra tables, and replication details
- provide variable-construction detail
- document sample construction and supplementary specifications
- include placebo tests, extra figures, and supporting institutional detail when relevant
- record replication or data-access notes if needed

The appendix should support the paper, not hide indispensable identification logic or core sample definitions.

---

## Working Rule

A strong paper structure should let a careful reader answer these questions quickly:

1. what is the question?  
2. why does it matter?  
3. what variation identifies the effect?  
4. on what sample and with what variables?  
5. where is the main evidence shown?  
6. what is baseline, and what is supporting evidence?  

If the structure makes those questions hard to answer, the paper likely needs reorganization even if each section is individually well written.
