# Economics and Agricultural Economics Paper Quality Criteria

Use this file to prioritize which papers deserve close reading.

The goal is not to rank papers by prestige alone.  
The goal is to identify which papers are most useful for:

- understanding a research question
- learning a credible design
- studying good empirical writing
- avoiding common pitfalls
- informing a new project

A paper is worth close reading when it is not only interesting, but also useful for design, interpretation, or writing.

---

## Core Screening Principle

When deciding whether a paper deserves close reading, ask:

1. Is the design credible enough to learn from?
2. Is the data actually informative for the claim?
3. Is the contribution stated precisely?
4. Is the paper written clearly enough to reverse-engineer?
5. Is it useful for the new project?

Do not rely only on:

- journal prestige
- citation counts
- abstract-level novelty claims

A highly cited paper may still be unhelpful for your project if its design, setting, or empirical discipline do not transfer.

---

## Dimensions

### 1. Identification Credibility

Ask:

- what assumption does the result hinge on?
- how believable is that assumption?
- is the identifying variation explicit?
- do the diagnostics actually address the main threat?
- does the paper’s language match the strength of the design?

Good signs:

- assumptions are stated clearly
- threats are acknowledged directly
- diagnostics are design-matched
- interpretation is disciplined rather than inflated

Warning signs:

- strong causal claims with weak identification discussion
- robustness checks that do not target the real threat
- event-study or IV language that sounds stronger than the design supports

---

### 2. Data Value

Ask:

- is the data unique, comprehensive, or policy-relevant?
- does the data support the stated claim?
- are treatment, outcomes, and sample clearly measurable?
- is the data structure well matched to the design?

Good signs:

- data directly fit the research question
- sample and variable construction are transparent
- the data support the actual claim, not just a weaker proxy claim
- the paper is clear about coverage and limits

Warning signs:

- impressive data that do not identify the key claim
- unclear sample construction
- variables that are central to the argument but weakly measured

---

### 3. Contribution Precision

Ask:

- is the paper clear about what is new?
- does it separate contribution from marketing?
- does it distinguish direct evidence from broader framing?
- does it explain why the paper matters without overselling novelty?

Good signs:

- the contribution is narrow, specific, and believable
- the paper distinguishes question, design, data, and finding contributions
- novelty claims are proportional to the evidence

Warning signs:

- vague “first to” claims
- literature gap claims that do not describe what the literature already knows
- methodological packaging substituting for real contribution

---

### 4. Writing and Table Clarity

Ask:

- can you trace the argument quickly?
- are tables and notes interpretable?
- do the results map cleanly onto the design?
- can you tell what each table is doing without reverse-engineering the code?

Good signs:

- introduction, design, results, and appendix fit together clearly
- table notes disclose sample, FE, clustering, and special rules
- the prose matches the tables closely
- appendix material supports rather than rescues the paper

Warning signs:

- hard-to-follow specification progression
- unclear table notes
- headline claims not traceable to a specific table or figure
- appendix hiding core design details

---

### 5. Relevance for the New Project

Ask:

- does it use a comparable design?
- does it study a comparable setting?
- does it reveal a useful empirical pitfall or pattern?
- does it offer a useful way to frame contribution, robustness, or mechanism?
- does it provide a reusable table, appendix, or writing pattern?

Good signs:

- comparable treatment variation or identification logic
- comparable data structure or policy setting
- useful pitfalls, robustness logic, or writing patterns
- transferable figure, table, or appendix design

Warning signs:

- topically related but methodologically irrelevant
- too far from the intended setting or estimand
- theoretically interesting but not useful for empirical design or paper construction

---

## Extra Value Signals

A paper often deserves especially close reading if it offers one or more of the following:

- a clean way to explain identifying assumptions
- a well-designed event-study, IV, or RDD presentation
- a transparent sample-construction section
- a strong robustness structure tied to threats
- a useful mechanism-writing pattern
- a helpful response-letter or appendix discipline
- a careful distinction between main findings and supporting evidence

---

## Red Flags

A paper may deserve only light reading if:

- the identification strategy is unclear
- the result relies on a very fragile assumption with little discussion
- the contribution claim is broader than the evidence
- the tables are hard to decode
- the setting is too far from your project to transfer usefully
- the paper is famous but not practically useful for your design, writing, or revision needs

---

## Working Rule

Prioritize close reading for papers that are strong on at least three of the following:

- identification credibility
- data value
- contribution precision
- writing and table clarity
- relevance for the new project

If a paper is weak on most of these dimensions, it may still be citeable, but it is probably not worth deep reverse-engineering.
