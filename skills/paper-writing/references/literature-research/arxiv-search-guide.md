# Economics and Agricultural Economics Literature Search Guide

This file keeps its old path for compatibility, but the active workflow is economics-first rather than arXiv-first.

Use this guide when searching literature for empirical economics and agricultural economics projects, especially for literature reviews, project design, identification mapping, and citation verification.

The goal is not just to find papers.  
The goal is to identify:

- what the literature already knows
- what designs have been used
- what data sources are feasible
- where the evidence is incomplete, conflicting, or weakly identified

---

## Default Search Order

Use sources in this order unless the project clearly requires something else:

1. `Google Scholar` for broad discovery
2. `RePEc / IDEAS` for economics metadata, versions, and related-paper tracing
3. `National Bureau of Economic Research` for working papers
4. publisher pages and journal sites for published versions
5. leading economics and agricultural economics field-journal sites when relevant

Do **not** default to `arXiv-first`.

Use `arXiv` only when:

- the citation is genuinely outside economics / agricultural economics
- the work is cross-disciplinary and no better primary source is available
- the user explicitly wants arXiv-based search

---

## Preferred Journal and Source Families

### General economics

Prefer published versions from sources such as:

- `American Economic Review`
- `Quarterly Journal of Economics`
- `Journal of Political Economy`
- `Econometrica`
- `Review of Economic Studies`
- `Economic Journal`
- `Journal of Public Economics`
- `Journal of Human Resources`
- `American Economic Journal: Applied Economics`
- `American Economic Journal: Economic Policy`
- `American Economic Journal: Microeconomics`
- `American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics`

### Agricultural economics and related field journals

Prefer published versions from sources such as:

- `American Journal of Agricultural Economics`
- `Applied Economic Perspectives and Policy`
- `Agricultural Economics`
- `European Review of Agricultural Economics`
- `Journal of Agricultural Economics`
- `Food Policy`

### Working-paper sources

Use carefully and track publication status:

- `National Bureau of Economic Research`
- `RePEc`
- `IDEAS`
- `SSRN`
- other recognized working-paper series when relevant

## Search Questions

For each search task, ask:

- what is the exact research question?
- what identification strategies have already been used?
- what data sources recur in the literature?
- where do findings disagree?
- which papers provide direct evidence versus only background or related logic?
- what remains unresolved for the new project?

A literature search should not stop at “papers found.”  
It should move toward a structured map of the evidence.

---

## Minimum Extraction Fields

For each useful paper, record at least:

- citation metadata
- publication status
- identification strategy
- data source
- setting / institutional context
- main finding
- limitations relevant to the new project
- whether the paper provides direct evidence or indirect support

When relevant, also record:

- treatment definition
- outcome family
- unit of observation
- sample period
- external-validity limits
- whether the paper is a working-paper version or a published version

---

## Version Discipline

Economics papers often exist in multiple versions.

Always check:

- whether a working paper has a published journal version
- whether title, year, and venue belong to the same version
- whether the result you want to cite appears in the version you are using

Preferred rule:

- use the published version when it exists and is substantively the right source
- retain the working-paper version only when it is still unpublished or when the working-paper version itself is the relevant object

Do not mix:

- working-paper title with journal metadata
- working-paper year with publication year
- claims from one version with bibliographic details from another

---

## Search Output Goal

A strong literature search should produce material that can directly support:

- `literature-review.md`
- `research-design.md`
- `identification-map.md`
- `references.bib`
- citation verification or response-letter revision when needed

The search is successful when it helps clarify:

1. what the literature establishes  
2. what methods dominate  
3. what data are feasible  
4. where the new project can contribute credibly  

---

## Working Rule

Use `Google Scholar` for discovery.  
Use `RePEc / IDEAS`, `National Bureau of Economic Research`, publisher pages, and journal sites for version control, metadata verification, and published-paper confirmation.
