# Tone Guidelines

The goal is to sound professional, transparent, and calm.

## Preferred Tone

- respectful
- direct
- evidence-based
- specific about manuscript changes

## Good Phrases

- "We thank the referee for this helpful comment."
- "We agree that the previous draft did not explain this clearly enough."
- "To address this concern, we now ..."
- "We added Table A.X and revised Section Y accordingly."
- "We respectfully do not adopt this suggestion because ..."

## Avoid

- "The referee is wrong."
- "Obviously"
- vague promises without a concrete manuscript change
- overstating what a new robustness check proves

## Economics-Specific Reminder

If the referee points to an identification or data concern, respond with design logic and manuscript changes, not just rhetorical reassurance.
